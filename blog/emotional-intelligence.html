Emotional Intelligence

What is EQ?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) refers to the capacity to recognise, control, and utilise emotions effectively to reduce stress, communicate well, empathise with others, handle difficulties, and resolve conflicts. EQ aids in establishing better relationships, attaining success in academics and careers, and accomplishing personal and professional objectives. It also enables individuals to connect with their emotions, translate intentions into actions, and make informed decisions aligned with their priorities.

Academics argue there are five key attributes that constitute EQ:

    Self-Awareness
    Self-Regulation
    Motivation
    Empathy
    Social Skills

The Importance of EQ

Emotional intelligence fosters and builds stronger relationships, academic and professional success, and the fulfillment of personal and career aspirations. It facilitates emotional connection, intention-driven action, and informed decision-making for personal objectives.

In summary, it makes you a more relatable person, one who creates and fosters a safe space for others to be vulnerable and helps you achieve goals through building a strong support network around you. You need it to survive.

How to Increase Your EQ

By exercising mindfulness and being completely present, and engaged in conversation. Opening ourselves to experiencing new challenges and things that push us out of our comfort zone.
